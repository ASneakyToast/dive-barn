Yorkville Schoolhouse Ranch

A 2,000-acre historic property in the heart of Mendocino County

Nestled in the rolling hills of Mendocino County, Yorkville Schoolhouse Ranch spans 2,000 acres of California landscape. This historic property serves as the backdrop for Dive Barn, an annual gathering of art, music, and community.

Read on for a brief history of the property.

Current Stewards

Eric Carlson and Barbara Henderson

Eric and Barbara purchased Yorkville Schoolhouse Ranch in 2022, renaming it the Yorkville Schoolhouse Ranch to emphasize the important role that heritage building played in forming the surrounding community. As the current stewards of this historic land, they are committed to preserving its natural beauty and rich history while sharing it with the community through events like Dive Barn.

A Brief History

From schoolhouse to cultural gathering place

Early 1860s

The One-Room Schoolhouse

The one-room schoolhouse, currently residing on the south-east side of the property, was built in the early 1860s on land donated by prominent early settler Silas E. Gaskill. The original location was just across the highway from the entrance to the Jim Hill Ranch.

1865

The Gaskill Mystery

In a dramatic turn of events, Silas E. Gaskill was arrested on suspicion of murder in 1865. He was released on his own recognizance for $1,000, but forfeited the bail, fled the country, and was never heard from again—leaving behind one of the valley's enduring mysteries.

1919-2008

The Johnson Ranch Era

Glenn Johnson began building the 2,000-acre ranch in 1919 by acquiring and combining smaller properties and homesteads. Later, his son Stanley continued expanding the operation. The Johnsons ran sheep and cattle on the ranch until Stanley retired in 2008, ending nearly 90 years of family stewardship.

1926

Moving the Schoolhouse

The schoolhouse was moved to its current location in 1926 and continued to serve as the Yorkville grammar and middle school until 1947, when California passed a law requiring all schools to have flush toilets—marking the end of its educational era.

2012-2022

The Acorn Ranch

Peter and Mimi Buckley purchased the ranch from the Johnsons in 2012, renaming it The Acorn Ranch. The Buckleys imported a herd of Cinta Senese pigs from Italy—a rare and ancient Tuscan breed prized by charcuterie makers—and raised them on the property. However, some pigs escaped and bred with wild boars, contributing to Northern California's wild pig population. These are the pigs you may encounter on the property today.

2022-Present

Yorkville Schoolhouse Ranch

Eric Carlson and Barbara Henderson purchased the ranch from the Buckleys in 2022, renaming it the Yorkville Schoolhouse Ranch to emphasize the important role that heritage building played in forming the surrounding community. Today, the property continues its legacy as a gathering place, now hosting Dive Barn and other cultural events that celebrate creativity, community, and connection to the land.

Preserving the Legacy

Eric and Barbara are committed to preserving both the natural beauty and historical significance of this special place. Through events like Dive Barn, they're ensuring that Yorkville Schoolhouse Ranch continues its long tradition as a gathering place for community, creativity, and connection.

From its origins with the mysterious Silas Gaskill through nearly a century of ranching by the Johnson family, to the innovative agricultural ventures of the Buckleys, and now as a venue for artistic and cultural events, the ranch continues to evolve while honoring its rich heritage.
