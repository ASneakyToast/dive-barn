Yorkville Schoolhouse Ranch

A 2,000-acre historic property in the heart of Mendocino County

Nestled in the rolling hills of Mendocino County, Yorkville Schoolhouse Ranch spans 2,000 acres of pristine California landscape. This historic property serves as the perfect backdrop for Dive Barn's annual gathering, where art, music, and community come together under endless skies.

The ranch combines natural beauty with rich history, offering festival-goers a unique experience far from the noise of urban life. Ancient oak trees, rolling meadows, and historic structures‚Äîincluding a one-room schoolhouse from the 1860s and a tobacco barn from the 1800s‚Äîcreate an atmosphere that's both timeless and magical.

Current Stewards

Eric Carlson and Barbara Henderson

Eric and Barbara purchased Yorkville Schoolhouse Ranch in 2022, rechristening it from its previous name, The Acorn Ranch. As the current stewards of this historic land, they are committed to preserving its natural beauty and rich history while sharing it with the community through events like Dive Barn.

Their vision aligns perfectly with Dive Barn's mission of bringing people together through art and music, continuing the property's long tradition as a gathering place for the community.

A Rich History

From schoolhouse to cultural gathering place

1800s

The Tobacco Barn

The tobacco barn was built sometime in the 1800s and was used as a tobacco drying barn by a Hopland cigar company. After the cigar company closed, it was repurposed as a hop drying barn, reflecting the changing agricultural needs of the region.

Early 1860s

The One-Room Schoolhouse

The one-room schoolhouse was built in the early 1860s on land donated by prominent early settler Silas E. Gaskill, who was considered one of the valley's "best citizens." The original location was just across the highway from the entrance to the Jim Hill Ranch.

1865

The Gaskill Mystery

In a dramatic turn of events, Silas E. Gaskill was arrested on suspicion of murder in 1865. He was released on his own recognizance for $1,000, but forfeited the bail, fled the country, and was never heard from again‚Äîleaving behind one of the valley's enduring mysteries.

1919-2008

The Johnson Ranch Era

Glenn Johnson began building the 2,000-acre ranch in 1919 by acquiring and combining smaller properties and homesteads. Later, his son Stanley continued expanding the operation. The Johnsons ran sheep and cattle on the ranch until Stanley retired in 2008, ending nearly 90 years of family stewardship.

1926

Moving the Schoolhouse

The schoolhouse was moved to its current location in 1926 and continued to serve as the Yorkville grammar and middle school until 1947, when California passed a law requiring all schools to have flush toilets‚Äîmarking the end of its educational era.

2012-2022

The Acorn Ranch

Peter and Mimi Buckley purchased the ranch from the Johnsons in 2012, renaming it The Acorn Ranch. The Buckleys imported a herd of Cinta Senese pigs from Italy‚Äîa rare and ancient Tuscan breed prized by charcuterie makers‚Äîand raised them on the property.

2022-Present

Yorkville Schoolhouse Ranch

Eric Carlson and Barbara Henderson purchased the ranch from the Buckleys in 2022, rechristening it Yorkville Schoolhouse Ranch. Today, the property continues its legacy as a gathering place, now hosting Dive Barn and other cultural events that celebrate creativity, community, and connection to the land.
